    • Extensive and Intensive Properties
    Lesson 4
    matter possesses observable qualities that can be categorized into two types: extensive properties and intensive properties. Extensive properties, such as mass and volume, are proportional to the amount of matter present in a sample and cannot be used to characterize a type of matter on their own. On the other hand, intensive properties, like density and melting point, are independent of the amount of matter and are unique to each type of substance.


    The ratio of two extensive properties always results in an intensive property. For example, the ratio of mass to volume gives us the density of a substance, which is an intensive property. Intensive properties are crucial in characterizing matter because each type of substance has a distinct set of intensive properties that differentiate it from others.


    Intensive properties can be identified through simple observations, such as color, solubility, and acidity. While many intensive properties may depend on variables like temperature and pressure, the way they change with these variables can also be considered as intensive properties.
