    • Naming Acids and Bases
    Lesson 14
    Acids are compounds that produce hydrogen ions (H+) when dissolved in water. They are named based on the anion they contain. If the anion ends in -ide, the acid is named with the prefix hydro- and the suffix -ic. For example, HCl (hydrogen chloride) produces chloride ions (Cl-) and is named hydrochloric acid. If the anion ends in -ate, the acid is named with the suffix -ic. For example, HNO3 (nitric acid) produces nitrate ions (NO3-) and is named nitric acid. If the anion ends in -ite, the acid is named with the suffix -ous. For example, H2SO3 (sulfurous acid) produces sulfite ions (SO32-) and is named sulfurous acid.

    Examples:
    1. HClO4 - perchloric acid
    2. H2SO4 - sulfuric acid
    3. HBr - hydrobromic acid
    4. H2CO3 - carbonic acid
    5. HNO2 - nitrous acid

    Naming Bases:
    Bases are compounds that produce hydroxide ions (OH-) when dissolved in water. They are named by using the name of the cation followed by the word "hydroxide." For example, NaOH (sodium hydroxide) produces sodium ions (Na+) and hydroxide ions (OH-) and is named sodium hydroxide.

    Examples:
    1. Ca(OH)2 - calcium hydroxide
    2. NH4OH - ammonium hydroxide
    3. LiOH - lithium hydroxide
    4. Mg(OH)2 - magnesium hydroxide
