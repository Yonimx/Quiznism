    • Nature of Matter
    Lesson 1
    Matter - is defined as something that can be weighed and takes up space. Therefore, matter has both mass and volume. It is something we are able to sense and it is made up of tiny atoms. Both living and non-living entities are made out of matter. Water, air, ice, paper, iron, etc are examples of matter.


    Nature of Matter :

    • Atoms that have protons, neutrons, electrons and a nucleus consist of matter.

    • In general, it can exist in four states: liquid, solid, gaseous, and plasma.

    • The universe is a matter.

    • Not all energy is made up of matter. Heat, music, electricity, etc. are a few examples.
    • Matter is what we can see, hear, feel, touch, or taste.

    • It has its own mass and takes up space.

    • We can categorize matter based on its physical and chemical properties.

    •Two types of solids are crystalline and amorphous.

    • Pure substances and mixtures are used to classify the chemical properties of materials. The two types of mixtures are homogeneous mixtures and heterogeneous mixtures. Pure substances, however, are further divided into elements and compounds. Metals and non-metals are two types of elements, while organic and inorganic substances are two types of compounds.

    • Different kinds of matter have different kinds of atoms.

    • The density and inertia of all matter are different.

    • The interparticle force or the intermolecular force is what draws matter particles together.
