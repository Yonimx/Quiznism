    • Physical and Chemical Properties
    Lesson 3
    Physical properties - Physical properties are those that can be measured or observed without affecting the chemical composition of a material. Extensive and intensive properties are the types of physical properties. Ex: Density, colour, hardness, melting, etc.

    Chemical properties - The ability to change from one type of matter into another and a property of a substance relating to its chemical reactivity.
    Ex: Acidity, flammability, toxicity etc.

    Physical changes - A change in matter's properties or qualities without a change in the components' of chemical identities. It does not transform a substance into one that is completely different.

    Ex: changes in the size or shape Chemical changes - It is also known as "chemical reactions". It creates one or more types of matter that are different from the forms of matter that were present before the transformation.

    Reactants are the "ingredients" in a reaction, while products are the outputs of that reaction. Ex: Photosynthesis, rust, baking, digestion, and etc.
