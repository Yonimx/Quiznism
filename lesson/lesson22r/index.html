    • Volume and Pressure: Boyle's Law
    Lesson 22
    Boyle's law, also known as Mariotte's law, is a relationship describing how a gas will compress and expand at a constant temperature. The pressure (p) of a given quantity of gas changes inversely with its volume (v) at constant temperature, according to this empirical connection, which was established by the physicist Robert Boyle in 1662. In equation form, this means that pv = k, a constant. The French physicist Edme Mariotte (1676) also found the connection. Mathematically this can be written:

    P1V1 = P2V2
