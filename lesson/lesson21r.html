    • Lesson 21: Volume and Temperature
    Charles' law states that a gas's volume is determined by multiplying a fixed amount by its Kelvin-scale temperature.The relationship between a given amount of gas's volume and temperature at Charles' law is named in honour of the French physicist and inventor of balloon flying, Jacques Alexandre César Charles. According to Charles' law, a gas's volume is directly inversely proportional to its temperature on the kelvin scale when the gas is at its ambient temperature. The pressure remains constant.
