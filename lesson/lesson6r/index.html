    • Methods of Separating Mixtures into their Component Substances
    Lesson 6
    Separation of Mixtures - It is a process where you will separate the two different substances in a solution. There are many ways to separate mixtures, such as evaporation, distillation, filtration and chromatography.

    1. Chromatography - the separation of a mixture by passing it through a medium in order to separate it. wherein the constituents flow at different speeds, either as a solution or suspension, or as a vapour. Thin-layer chromatography is a method of separating and identifying coloured or colorable materials, particularly pigments. It can be liquid chromatography, gas chromatography, ion-exchange chromatography and affinity chromatography.

    2. Distillation - A separation technique used to separate components of a liquid combination using a heating and cooling process that takes use of variations in the volatility of each component.

    3. Evaporation - It can be used to separate components of a combination containing a dissolved solid in a liquid. The liquid is evaporated, which means it is converted from a liquid to a gaseous form. This often requires the use of heat. The solid is all that remains after the liquid has entirely evaporated.

    4. Filtration - a process for separating the components of a combination that contains an undissolved solid in a liquid. Filtration can be done cold or hot, with gravity or with a vacuum, with the use of a funnel or with a simple glass funnel. The precise method utilized is determined by the objective of the filtering, whether it is to isolate a solid from a mixture or to remove contaminants from a mixture.
