    Acid-Base Reactions

    -Both biochemistry and industrial chemistry depend on acid-base reactions. In addition, we frequently come across acids or bases in our houses, grocery stores, and pharmacies. For instance, antacids are bases and aspirin is an acid (acetylsalicylic acid). Actually, every home cook who has made mayonnaise or marinated fish with lemon juice has performed an acid-base interaction. Let's first define some of the qualities of such reactions before talking about their attributes, bases and acids.
