    • Significant Figures in Calculations
    Lesson 8
    Exact numbers - Exact numbers are completely certain. They have an endless amount of significant digits, if you like. We suppose that the numbers we use to define and count other quantities are precise with an infinite range of significant values.
    Ex: 1.000000000000000


    Rules for Significant Figures

    1. All non-zero numbers are significant. Ex: 23.3

    2. Zeros between two non-zero digits are significant. Ex: 3033

    3. Leading zeros are not significant. Ex: 0.0025

    4.Trailing zeros to the right of the decimal are significant. Ex: 21.00

    Leading Zeros - Zeros that you see before a number.
    Ex: 0056
    - The zeros just indicate that this particular number has no bigger values, thus they can be ignored.

    Trailing Zeros - The zeros at the end of a number. It can be ignored if you see them in a decimal number.
    Ex: 0.004500
    - You can disregard the zeros after the five because they don't provide any extra data.

    Addition/Subtraction of Significant Figures
    - When adding/subtracting, the answer should have the same number of decimal places as the limiting term.
    Ex:
    5365.999
    - 234.66706
    5131.33194 - then round off the answer =5131.332

    The rule for multiplication and division is to count the significant figures in each number being multiplied or divided, and then limit the significant figures in the answer to the lowest count of significant figures.
    Ex:
    41.45 x 101.25 =4,196.8125
