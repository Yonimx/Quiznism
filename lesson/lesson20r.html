    • Mathematical Relationship between Pressure, Volume and Temperature
    Lesson 20
    - The late 1600s saw Guillaume Amonton, a French physicist, discover Amonton's law. Amonton's law states that if a gas's volume remains constant, its pressure will rise as its temperature rises. How come this is the case? Heat increases the energy in a gas. The more often its particles collide and move, the more pressure the gas is under. It asserts that, while the volume is held constant, the pressure of a given amount of gas is precisely proportional to its temperature on the kelvin scale. Mathematically, this can be written:
